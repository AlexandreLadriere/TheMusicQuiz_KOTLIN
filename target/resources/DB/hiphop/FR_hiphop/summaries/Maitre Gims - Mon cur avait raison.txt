Carcassonne is a French commune, prefecture of the department of Aude in the region Occitania.
At the last census of 2016, the town had 45,941 inhabitants called Carcassonnais. Carcassonne is the main city of Carcassonne Agglo 105,104 inhabitants (2010), the urban area of ​​Carcassonne 98,318 inhabitants (2013) and its urban unit with 49,257 inhabitants (2013).
Occupied since the Neolithic, Carcassonne is located in the plain of Aude between two major roads linking the Atlantic to the Mediterranean Sea and the Massif Central to the Pyrenees.
The city is known for the city of Carcassonne, medieval architectural complex restored by Viollet-le-Duc in the nineteenth century and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1997.
