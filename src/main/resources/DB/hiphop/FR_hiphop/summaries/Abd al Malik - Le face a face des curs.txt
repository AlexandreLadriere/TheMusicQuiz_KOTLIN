The case of Malik Oussekine is a case of French police violence resulting from the death of Malik Oussekine, on the night of December 5, 1986 to December 6, 1986 in Paris, after several weeks of student protest against the Devaquet university reform project.
The day after the death, on Saturday morning, the students are received at the Ministry of the Interior and organize a silent march, while the Minister Delegate Alain Devaquet, presents his resignation.
Two days later, the Devaquet Bill is withdrawn. Four days later, other silent marches take place everywhere in France, Wednesday, December 10, the unions of employees calling to go there and bringing their services of order.
Two of the three policemen who fatally beat Malik Oussekine will then be tried and sentenced.
