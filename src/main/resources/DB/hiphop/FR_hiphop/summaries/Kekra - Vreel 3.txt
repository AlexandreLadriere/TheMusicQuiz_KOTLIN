Jérôme Kerviel, born on January 11, 1977 in Pont-l'Abbé (Finistère), is, from 2005 to 2008, a French market operator, employee of Société Générale. It is a major player in Société Générale's losses discovered in January 2008, as a result of the liquidation of its holdings on equity index futures contracts, which amounted to around 50 billion euros at that time. euros.
For breach of trust, forgery and use of forgery and fraudulent introduction of data into a computer system, he is sentenced to 3 years in prison and 2 years suspended. After 5 months, his sentence is arranged in freedom under electronic bracelet.
Following the liquidation of his positions, Jérôme Kerviel is held liable for all losses recorded by the banking group, or 4.9 billion euros. The judgment rendered by the Court of Cassation in March 2014 shares the responsibilities between Jérôme Kerviel and Société Générale and removes the amount of 4.9 billion euros claimed in damages; the court of appeal of Versailles, ruling on the civil interests sentenced him on September 23, 2016 to pay 1 million euros in damages to Société Générale.
Earlier in 2016, Societe Generale had been ordered to pay Jerome Kerviel 455 000 euros in damages by the industrial tribunal of Paris for dismissal without cause real and serious under vexatious conditions.
In December 2018, the Paris Court of Appeal quashed the decision of the industrial tribunal.
