Christophe Rocancourt, born July 16, 1967 in Honfleur (Calvados), is a French crook.
In 2000, he gained notoriety when he was incarcerated in the United States, where he had extorted money from various personalities, some famous (such as Mickey Rourke or Michel Polnareff), using a dozen false identities, including pretending to be a film producer.
Author of a successful autobiographical book, he returned to France after his release in October 2005 and enjoys a status of celebrity media.
In 2012, he was sentenced for abuse of weakness on the filmmaker Catherine Breillat; in 2014, he is locked up in a new case. In February 2015, he is indicted in the context of the case of theft of cocaine at the headquarters of the Judicial Police of Paris.
