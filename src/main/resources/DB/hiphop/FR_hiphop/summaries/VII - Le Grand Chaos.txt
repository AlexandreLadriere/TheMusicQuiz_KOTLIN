Charles Miles Manson (born Maddox), born November 12, 1934 in Cincinnati, Ohio, USA, and died November 19, 2017 in Bakersfield, California, is an American criminal.
Leader of a community called "Manson Family" or "Manson Family" in the midst of the hippie era in the late 1960s, he became famous for a series of assassinations in the Los Angeles area in 1969. He was convicted in 1971 of the high-profile murder of actress Sharon Tate, wife of eight-month-old director Roman Polanski and four of her friends. He did not commit the crimes himself, but was recognized as the sponsor. He was sentenced to death and commuted to life imprisonment.
