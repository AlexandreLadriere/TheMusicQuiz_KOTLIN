Scabies (gall, "gall", or scabies, "scabies") is an infectious skin disease caused by a microscopic mite-like parasite (Sarcoptes scabiei). According to the subtype of this parasite, human scabies, specific for humans, and specific scales of other mammals are distinguished. It is a benign contagious condition (not serious). The main symptom in humans is itching. The lesional profile is that of perivascular dermatitis. Depending on the case, the diagnosis may be made on clinical examination or may require a sample. The treatment uses drugs used orally or cutaneously. Often associated is the treatment of the environment and the decontamination of the environment.
